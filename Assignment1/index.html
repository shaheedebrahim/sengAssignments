An Introduction To Competitive Programming

What is Competitive Programming?

Competitive programming is a mind sport usually held over the Internet or a local network, involving participants trying to program according to provided specifications. Source

This explanation is complex and not entirely clear. To put it in laymens terms, competitive programming is a contest held for a specified amount of time where participants are challenged to solve specific problems within the time limit.

What Are The Rules?

The rules to competitive programming vary by contest but since the most reknown contest is the ACM-ICPC (Association for Computing Machinery - International Collegiate Programming Contest) and most contests follow those rules we shall use them as our baselines.
