Online Judges

Which Online Judges to Use?

There are many online judges on the internet and I will attempt to show you the weaknesses, strengths and points of interest of each. While the following comparison will not include every single judge, it will cover the most used.

To compare the judges several categories will be used:

  1. Problem Tags: A yes in this category means that the online judge has tags to identify the type of problem (dynammic programming, graph, etc)
  2. Difficulty Levels: A yes in this category means that the online judge has some way of identifying the difficulty level of a problem
  3. Accessibility: This category discusses whether the judges website or platform is easily accessibile and its UI is easy to use
  4. Speed: This category measures the speed of the judge when submitting a problem
  5. Contests: This section asks whether the platform has contests that a user can join and the format of these contests
  6. Unique Attributes: The final category highlights any unique points of interest that the judge has that is worth highlighting
